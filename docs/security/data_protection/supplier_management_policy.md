# Supplier Management Policy

This policy ensures that third-party suppliers handling services or data for the organization operate securely and meet required standards.

## Purpose
The goal is to reduce risks from external suppliers by ensuring they are properly evaluated, approved, and monitored throughout their relationship with the organization.

## Scope
Applies to all external suppliers, including cloud providers, software vendors, consultants, and service partners that may impact security or operations.

## Supplier Classification
Suppliers are categorized by risk level:
- **High risk:** Access to sensitive data or critical systems.  
- **Medium risk:** Limited access but relevant to business operations.  
- **Low risk:** No direct impact on systems or data.

## Supplier Evaluation
Before onboarding, suppliers are assessed on:
- Security measures and certifications (e.g., ISO 27001).  
- Data protection practices.  
- Reliability and service capability.

High-risk suppliers require stricter review and approval.

## Contract Requirements
Contracts must define:
- Security and confidentiality obligations.  
- Data handling rules and breach notification.  
- Service expectations and responsibilities.

## Monitoring
Suppliers are periodically reviewed to ensure continued compliance, especially those classified as high risk.

## Termination
If a supplier fails to meet requirements, access is removed and data must be returned or securely deleted.

## Responsibilities
Management, security teams, and procurement work together to assess suppliers, review risks, and maintain compliance.
