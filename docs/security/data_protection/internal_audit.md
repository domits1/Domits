# Internal Audit Policy

This policy defines how the organization conducts internal audits of its Information Security Management System (ISMS) to ensure effectiveness, compliance, and continuous improvement.

## Purpose
The goal of internal audits is to verify that policies, procedures, and controls are properly implemented and functioning as intended. Audits help identify weaknesses and ensure compliance with ISO 27001 and internal requirements.

## Scope
This policy applies to all processes, departments, and systems within the ISMS scope.

## Audit Planning
Internal audits are performed at planned intervals. The audit plan is based on:
- Risk levels of processes and systems  
- Results of previous audits  
- Changes in technology or operations  

## Audit Execution
Audits are conducted by trained personnel who are independent from the areas being audited. They review documentation, interview staff, and test controls to assess effectiveness.

## Reporting
Findings are documented in an audit report that includes:
- Observations  
- Nonconformities  
- Recommendations for improvement  

Reports are shared with management for review.

## Corrective Actions
Identified issues must be addressed within agreed timelines. Corrective actions are tracked until resolved to ensure continuous improvement of the ISMS.

## Responsibilities
- **Audit Lead:** Plans and coordinates audits.  
- **Auditors:** Perform independent evaluations.  
- **Management:** Reviews results and ensures corrective actions are completed.
